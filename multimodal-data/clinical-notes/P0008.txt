History of Present Illness:

The patient is a 52-year-old male with a history of Type 2 Diabetes
(diagnosed 12 years ago), hypertension, obesity (BMI 34.8), and
peripheral neuropathy who presents with worsening fatigue over the past
2 months, intermittent blurred vision for the past 3 weeks, and
progressive bilateral foot numbness extending to the ankles.

He reports his blood glucose readings at home have been frequently above
250 mg/dL despite taking his medications. He admits to poor dietary
adherence and inconsistent exercise over the past 6 months due to foot
discomfort. He describes the numbness as a constant "tingling and
burning" sensation in both feet, worse at night, which has been
progressing proximally. He has also noticed a small non-healing ulcer on
his right great toe for the past 2 weeks.

He denies chest pain, dyspnea, palpitations, or syncope. He reports
mild bilateral ankle swelling that is worse at the end of the day. He
denies fevers, chills, weight loss, polyuria, or polydipsia beyond his
baseline. He has not had a dilated eye exam in over 2 years.

Current medications include metformin 1000 mg twice daily, glipizide 10
mg twice daily, lisinopril 20 mg daily, amlodipine 10 mg daily, and
atorvastatin 40 mg daily. He denies drug allergies. He is a non-smoker,
drinks alcohol rarely, and has a family history of Type 2 Diabetes
(mother and maternal grandmother) and myocardial infarction (father,
age 60).

Vitals: temperature 36.8 C, heart rate 82 bpm, respiratory rate 16/min,
blood pressure 152/94 mmHg, SpO2 98% on room air, BMI 34.8. Examination
