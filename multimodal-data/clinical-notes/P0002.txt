History of Present Illness:

The patient is a 34-year-old female who presents with a 5-day history of
fever (peak 39.2 C), productive cough with yellowish-green sputum, and
right-sided pleuritic chest pain that worsens with deep inspiration. She
also reports progressive dyspnea over the past 3 days, now occurring
with minimal exertion such as walking to the bathroom.

She has a known history of mild intermittent asthma, well-controlled
with an as-needed albuterol inhaler. She denies recent asthma
exacerbations. She reports decreased oral intake over the past 2 days
due to malaise and mild nausea, but denies vomiting or diarrhea.

She denies hemoptysis, night sweats, weight loss, recent travel, or
known sick contacts. No history of tuberculosis exposure. She is a
non-smoker and denies alcohol or recreational drug use. She works as a
schoolteacher. No recent hospitalizations or antibiotic use in the past
3 months. Immunizations are up to date including annual influenza
vaccine.

On presentation, she appears acutely ill. Vitals: temperature 38.8 C,
heart rate 112 bpm, respiratory rate 22/min, blood pressure 118/72 mmHg,
SpO2 94% on room air. Lung auscultation reveals crackles and bronchial
breath sounds over the right lower lobe with dullness to percussion.
