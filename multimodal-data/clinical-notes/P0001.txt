History of Present Illness:

The patient is a 58-year-old male who presents with a 2–3 week history
of intermittent chest discomfort occurring primarily with exertion. He
describes the sensation as a pressure-like tightness located
substernally, rated 4/10 in intensity, without radiation to the jaw,
neck, or arms. Episodes are triggered by moderate activity such as
climbing stairs or brisk walking and resolve within approximately 5
minutes of rest. He denies chest pain at rest.

The patient also reports mild shortness of breath during these episodes,
which improves with cessation of activity. He denies palpitations,
syncope, dizziness, diaphoresis, nausea, or recent viral illness. No
recent trauma or prolonged immobilization. He notes increased fatigue
over the past month but attributes this partially to decreased exercise
tolerance.

He reports adherence to his prescribed medications and denies recent
medication changes. No recent travel. No prior similar episodes of this
severity, though he recalls occasional mild chest tightness in the past
year that resolved spontaneously.
