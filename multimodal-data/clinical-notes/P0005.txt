History of Present Illness:

The patient is a 28-year-old female with no significant past medical
history who presents for evaluation of recurrent palpitations over the
past 2 months. She describes episodes of rapid heartbeat lasting 5 to
30 seconds, occurring 2-3 times per week. The episodes are often
triggered by stress at work or after consuming caffeine. She also notes
occasional lightheadedness during these episodes but denies syncope.

She denies chest pain, dyspnea, diaphoresis, or radiation of discomfort
to the neck, jaw, or arms. She has no history of thyroid disease,
anemia, or cardiac conditions. She denies fevers, weight changes, heat
or cold intolerance, tremor, or diarrhea.

She drinks 3-4 cups of coffee daily and reports moderate work-related
stress as a software engineer. She exercises regularly (running 3 times
per week) without limitation. She is a non-smoker, drinks alcohol
occasionally, and denies recreational drug use including stimulants. No
family history of sudden cardiac death, cardiomyopathy, or arrhythmias.

She has no known allergies and takes no regular medications aside from a
daily multivitamin.

Vitals: temperature 36.6 C, heart rate 78 bpm, respiratory rate 14/min,
blood pressure 110/68 mmHg, SpO2 99% on room air, BMI 22.4. Physical
examination is unremarkable. Heart sounds are regular rate and rhythm,
no murmurs, gallops, or rubs. Lungs are clear. No thyromegaly, tremor,
or peripheral edema.
