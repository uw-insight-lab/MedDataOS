History of Present Illness:

The patient is a 72-year-old female with a known history of heart
failure with reduced ejection fraction (last EF 30% six months ago),
chronic atrial fibrillation, chronic kidney disease stage 3, and
hypertension. She presents with progressive dyspnea over the past week,
now occurring at rest. She reports worsening bilateral lower extremity
edema and a 4 kg weight gain over the past 10 days despite adherence to
her diuretic regimen.

She describes three-pillow orthopnea and two episodes of paroxysmal
nocturnal dyspnea this week, waking her from sleep with air hunger. She
denies chest pain, palpitations beyond her baseline irregular rhythm,
syncope, or presyncope. She reports decreased urine output over the past
3 days and mild nausea with reduced appetite.

She notes a dietary indiscretion 2 weeks ago during a family gathering,
with higher sodium intake than usual. She confirms adherence to her
medications including furosemide 40 mg twice daily, carvedilol 12.5 mg
twice daily, losartan 50 mg daily (switched from lisinopril due to
prior angioedema), and apixaban 5 mg twice daily for atrial
fibrillation. She has a known allergy to ACE inhibitors (angioedema with
lisinopril).

On presentation, she is in moderate respiratory distress, sitting
upright. Vitals: temperature 36.9 C, heart rate 88 bpm (irregularly
irregular), respiratory rate 24/min, blood pressure 148/92 mmHg, SpO2
91% on room air. Examination reveals elevated JVP, bilateral crackles
to the mid-lung fields, an S3 gallop, a grade 3/6 holosystolic murmur
at the apex consistent with mitral regurgitation, and 3+ pitting edema
bilaterally to the knees.
