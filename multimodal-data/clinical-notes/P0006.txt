History of Present Illness:

The patient is a 67-year-old male with a history of COPD (GOLD stage 3,
FEV1 38% predicted on last spirometry), former smoker with 40 pack-year
history (quit 5 years ago), and osteoarthritis. He presents with a
3-day history of worsening dyspnea, increased sputum production, and
change in sputum character from white to purulent yellow-green.

He reports his baseline dyspnea is mMRC grade 2 (walks slower than
contemporaries on level ground due to breathlessness), but over the
past 3 days he has been breathless at rest and unable to complete
sentences without pausing. He has had increased use of his rescue
inhaler from 2-3 times daily to 8-10 times daily without adequate
relief. He denies fever, chills, hemoptysis, or chest pain. No recent
travel or known sick contacts, though his grandchild had an upper
respiratory infection last week.

He denies orthopnea, paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnea, or peripheral edema.
He has not had a COPD exacerbation requiring hospitalization in the past
year, though he was treated with a course of oral prednisone and
antibiotics by his PCP approximately 8 months ago.

Current maintenance medications include tiotropium 18 mcg inhaler daily,
fluticasone/salmeterol 250/50 mcg inhaler twice daily, albuterol
inhaler PRN, acetaminophen 500 mg as needed for joint pain, and
omeprazole 20 mg daily. He avoids aspirin due to a history of
aspirin-induced bronchospasm.

Vitals: temperature 37.4 C, heart rate 102 bpm, respiratory rate
26/min, blood pressure 142/88 mmHg, SpO2 88% on room air. Examination
reveals use of accessory muscles, prolonged expiratory phase, diffuse
expiratory wheezes bilaterally with diminished breath sounds at the
bases. Heart sounds are distant but regular. No peripheral edema.
