History of Present Illness:

The patient is a 63-year-old female with a history of hypertension and
hypothyroidism who presents with a 2-week history of intermittent
sensation of irregular heartbeat and associated fatigue. She first
noticed the irregular rhythm while checking her pulse during a routine
blood pressure measurement at home. She describes episodes of "fluttering"
in her chest lasting minutes to hours, sometimes associated with mild
exertional dyspnea that is new from her baseline.

She denies chest pain, syncope, presyncope, diaphoresis, or significant
lightheadedness. She has not experienced palpitations in the past. She
denies recent illness, fever, weight changes, heat intolerance, tremor,
or diarrhea that might suggest thyroid dysfunction. She reports good
adherence to her levothyroxine and notes her last TSH was checked 3
months ago and was within normal limits.

Her medical history includes hypertension managed with amlodipine 10 mg
daily with good control, and hypothyroidism managed with levothyroxine
75 mcg daily. She has a known allergy to iodine-based contrast media
(prior urticarial reaction during a CT scan). She is a non-smoker,
drinks one glass of wine on weekends, and walks 30 minutes daily. Family
history is notable for her mother having atrial fibrillation diagnosed
at age 70 and her father dying of a stroke at age 75.

Vitals: temperature 36.7 C, heart rate 92 bpm (irregular), respiratory
rate 16/min, blood pressure 138/84 mmHg, SpO2 98% on room air.
Examination reveals an irregularly irregular pulse. Heart auscultation
confirms irregularly irregular rhythm with variable intensity of S1, no
murmurs or gallops appreciated. Lungs clear bilaterally. No peripheral
edema or JVD.
