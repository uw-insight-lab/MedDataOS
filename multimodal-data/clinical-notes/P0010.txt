History of Present Illness:

The patient is a 41-year-old female presenting for a 3-month follow-up
visit after an anterior ST-elevation myocardial infarction (STEMI) that
was treated with primary percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) and
drug-eluting stent placement to the left anterior descending artery.
She has a history of coronary artery disease, depression, and
hyperlipidemia.

She reports gradual improvement in her exercise tolerance since the
event. She is currently able to walk 20-30 minutes on flat ground
without chest pain or significant dyspnea, compared to 5-10 minutes at
the time of discharge. She has been attending cardiac rehabilitation
twice weekly and tolerating the sessions well. She denies recurrent
chest pain, palpitations, syncope, or presyncope.

She reports adherence to her dual antiplatelet therapy and other
medications. She notes occasional fatigue and low motivation, which she
attributes to her depression, though she states her sertraline was
recently increased to 100 mg daily with some improvement. She denies
suicidal ideation. Sleep quality has been fair, with occasional
insomnia.

She has a known intolerance to lisinopril (persistent dry cough), and
was switched to losartan 50 mg daily during her hospitalization. Current
medications include aspirin 81 mg daily, clopidogrel 75 mg daily,
losartan 50 mg daily, metoprolol succinate 50 mg daily, rosuvastatin
40 mg daily, and sertraline 100 mg daily. She is a non-smoker and does
not drink alcohol. Family history is significant for premature coronary
artery disease (father with MI at age 45).

Vitals: temperature 36.7 C, heart rate 62 bpm, respiratory rate 14/min,
blood pressure 118/74 mmHg, SpO2 99% on room air, BMI 24.8. Examination
reveals regular rate and rhythm, normal S1 and S2, no murmurs, gallops,
or rubs. Lungs clear bilaterally. No peripheral edema. Radial access
